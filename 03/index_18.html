Alpha and Beta Diversity of the Bacterial Community

The flattening rarefaction curves confirmed that sequencing depth was sufficient (Fig. 4). The microbial community in the water environment was more diverse than the community identified in the intestinal content of Gibel carp and common carp.

The Shannon diversity and Chao richness of microbial communities in CT were lowest in both water and fish intestines compared to BT and BZT (Fig. 5). Bacterial communities in the water and intestines of the two carp species significantly differed according to NMDS analysis, based on Jaccard distances (ANOSIM, Global R=0.689 and P=.001).

Triplicates tended to cluster according to both sample origin and whether biofloc technology was applied. Microbiota of biofloc from BT and BZT clustered together, gut microbiota of Gibel carp from BT and BZT clustered together and gut microbiota of common carp from BT and BZT clustered together.

In contrast, bacterial communities in all sample types from CT were separated from biofloc treatments. The communities of Gibel carp and common carp from CT partially overlapped.
