Bacterial Diversity and Composition

High-throughput sequencing of 16S rRNA genes provided fine-scale resolution of the bacterial community in pond water. Bacterial diversity was promoted by BFT in all samples, especially in the intestines of gibel carp and common carp.

The composition of aquatic microorganisms is known to affect the microflora of fish (Gatesoupe, 1999) and, as could be seen in NMDS plots. The intestinal microflora in fish is likely both determined by living environment and fish species.

The living environment of Gibel carp and common carp were the same, while the intestinal microflora was different. Proteobacteria were the most abundant bacterial phylum in the untreated CT groups.

This phylum is common to the aquatic environment and plays an important role in the process of nutrient cycling and the mineralization of organic compounds (Kirchman, 2002; Kersters et al., 2006).

In BFT, Proteobacteria were also relatively abundant, though members of other phyla, namely Actinobacteria, Fusobateria, Chloroflexi and Saccharibacteria, increased.

The most abundant bacterial phyla identified in our experimental pond differed from those in a marine shrimp tank (Cardona et al., 2016), and resembled with those in fresh water tank (Wei et al., 2016).

In both BFT treatments, several water quality parameters was improved over time, such as total nitrogen and ammonia nitrogen, whereas these parameters increased in CT.

We could conclude that the supplement of glucose, and continuous aeration, had a significant effect on the structure of the pond microflora and that these changes affected water quality.

The water quality is significantly correlated with the composition of the environmental micro flora (Fig. 10). The bacterial community, nutritional composition, and structure of bioflocs was affected by the type of carbohydrate (Crab et al., 2010; Wei et al., 2016).

Wei et al. (2016) used a biofloc system, which was without animal, included tap water mixed with pond water, added glucose as carbon sources, found bioflocs were dominant in Proteobacteria and Bacteroidetes.

Proteobacteria and Acidobacteria predominated in the BFT treatments in the present study. This could be due to the original bacterial from the pond water and animal.
