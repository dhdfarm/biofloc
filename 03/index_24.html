Effects of Biofloc Treatment on Dissolved Nitrogen and Phosphorus

Both in the pond of aquaculture and the enclosure of present study, feed was added continually and increasingly. When glucose and air were added continuously, high carbohydrate and DO in the culture water enabled the assimilatory activity of aerobic heterotrophic bacteria to convert ammonium into bacterial biomass.

The concentration of dissolved nitrogen and phosphorous (TAN, NO3-N, TN and TP) were reduced in BFT treatments, demonstrating the capacity of BFT to mitigate the build-up of excess nitrogen and improve water quality. These findings are consistent with the previous studies (Xu and Pan, 2013; Zhao et al., 2014).

When carbohydrate added to the water (C/N=20), the change of dissolved nitrogen was mainly due to the increase in organic nitrogen.

The improvements occurred in water that was not exchanged, showing that biofloc treatment is compatible with fish health and relatively intense stimulation of microbial activity with constant additions of glucose and dietary supplements.

Based on the budgets of nitrogen, the inputs may be recycled via the biofloc. Nitrogen may eliminate from pond water by the process of denitrification and sedimentation. Based on the nitrogen budget, more nitrogen was removed from the system in the CT than BFT treatments.

Supplementary air and carbohydrate provided an idea environment for aerobic heterotrophic bacteria. In the past, researchers thought that of the rate of heterotrophic nitrification was low (Robertson and Kuenen, 1988).

While the effect of heterotrophic nitrification in the nature environment was noticed gradually.

Several strains isolated from wastewater treatment plants were capable of simultaneous heterotrophic nitrification and aerobic denitrification, including members of Acinetobacter, Alcaligenes, Thiosphaera, Rhodococcus and Pseudomonas can convert oxidized or nitrified ammonium to denitrified products such as NO, N2O and N2 under aerobic conditions (Chen et al., 2007; Honda et al., 1998; Robertson and Kuenen, 1990; Su et al., 2001).

In our study, the abundances of Acinetobacter spp., Rhodococcus sp., Bacillus sp., Pseudomonas spp. were lower in water of BFT than CT treatments. Thus, the nitrogen eliminated to air of BFT should be lower than that of CT due to the absence of the heterotrophic nitrifiers community in BFT.

DO concentration is an important factor that affects competition between PAB (polyphosphate accumulator) and GAB (glycogen accumulator), where PABs are favored at low DO levels.

Biological phosphorus removal processes systems operated at low DO levels to minimize the proliferation of GAOs (Carvalheira et al., 2014). In the present study, the DO was kept high to keep the fish healthy and this did, in fact, lead to higher growth of GABs and the near absence of PABs.

The absence of PABs may have impacted on the recycling of phosphorus in the water. The effect of low levels of PAB on phosphorus cycling in biofloc systems should be studied in the future. Nitrification, the conversion of ammonia to nitrate via nitrite, is an important way to remove ammonia.

It is a two-step process catalyzed by ammonia-oxidizing and nitrite-oxidizing bacteria (AOB and NOB). Aerobic ammonia-oxidation is often the first, rate-limiting step of nitrification.

A few previous studies (Rowan et al., 2003, Tawan et al., 2005, You et al., 2003) have debated whether Nitrosospira or Nitrosomonas is the predominant species in various wastewater treatment systems, while opposite results have been published (Siripong and Rittmann, 2007 and Thomas et al., 2010).

We found that Nitrosospira spp. and Nitrobacter spp. was abundant in water of aquaculture pond, Nitrospira spp. occupied the position of Nitrosomonas spp. in the treatment BFT.
