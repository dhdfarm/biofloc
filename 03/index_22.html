Discussion

In BFT aquaculture, heterotrophic bacteria is grown in flocs by adding organic carbon and continuing aeration to the culture pond. BFT system is based on the knowledge of conventional domestic wastewater treatment and applied in aquaculture (De Schryver et al., 2008).

Microbial biomass is grown on exogenous carbon source and fish excreta and feces resulting in a reuse of nitrogen and other components. The major driving force for floc formation is the growth of heterotrophic bacteria on supplied carbohydrates.

Our study demonstrated that BFT increased nitrogen accumulation in the form of fish biomass from 28% to 42% compared to conventional methods, supporting the results of Avnimelech (2009).

The limited knowledge on bacterial community of bifloc, function of dominant bacteria, budget of nutrients and gut microbiota of cultured animals limit the development of the technology.

Our study directly links the changes in the bacterial community composition in flocculated pond water to those changes of water qualities, nitrogen budget and those bacteria in the intestines of inhabitant fish.
