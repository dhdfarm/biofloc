Water Quality Determination and Water Sample Collection

Throughout the trial period, total ammonia nitrogen (TAN), pH, DO, water transparency (WT) were tested once every three days, other water quality parameters, nitrite nitrogen (NO2-N), nitrate nitrogen (NO3-N), total nitrogen (TN), total phosphorus (TP) and biofloc volume (BFV), were tested fortnightly.

At every sampling time point, 3 water samples were taken in each enclosure from 3 different places.

The 3 water samples were mixed and tested twice. The determination of TAN, NO2-N, NO3-N, TN, TP and TOC followed the methods described by the Water and Wastewater Monitoring Analysis (State Environmental Protection Administration of China, 2002).

BFV (suspended solids in CT) determination used Imhoff cones by registering the volume of sediment from 1000 mL of water sample after 20 min precipitation (Avnimelech, 1999). Water temperature and DO were monitored by using multiparameter water quality instruments (PRO ODO, YSI, USA).

At the end of the experiment, water was taken from each enclosure at 3 different places and completely mix to determine geosmin and 2- MIB concentrations and bacterial community.

Duplicate one-liter water samples with biofloc (suspended solid in CT) were collected and immediately transferred a clean sterile 1 L glass bottle leaving no headspace to avoid volatilization.

Samples were immediately preserved in an icebox packed with ice packs during transit to the laboratory. Each sample was filtered through four to five 1.2 μm pore size, 47mm diameter GF/C filters (Whatman, Norway).

Each set of filters was cut into fragments in mm-sized pieces with a disposable sterile scalpel blade. Microbial DNA was extracted from samples using the E.Z.N.A.Â® soil DNA Kit (Omega Bio-tek, Norcross, GA, U.S.) according to manufacturer's protocols.

The DNA extract from each set of filters were pooled to prepare one DNA pool per enclosures.
