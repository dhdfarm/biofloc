Bioinformatic Analyses of Microbial Community Composition and Diversity

Rarefaction curves were plotted for each sample to characterize sampling depth (Amato et al., 2013). Alpha-diversity analyses were calculated using the Mothur (Schloss et al., 2011), including community richness parameters (Chao) and community diversity parameters (Shannon).

Beta diversity measurements were calculated as described by Jiang et al. (2013). Non-metric Multidimensional Scaling (NMDS) plots based on Jaccard distance were utilized to visualize the separation of microbiota structure across different groups.

Statistical testing among variation in microbial community composition was carried out using the analysis of similarity (ANOSIM). P < .05 was considered statistically significant.

The relationships among microorganisms' populations and water quality parameters (temperature, pH, DO, ammonium, nitrite, nitrate, and phosphate) was examined by distance-based redundancy analysis (db-RDA).

Environmental factors were selected by VIF (Variance inflation factor), and the environmental factors with VIF > 10 were removed from the db-RDA analysis.

The analyses of NMDS, ANOSIM and db-RDA were performed with the R package “vegan: Community ecology” package (Oksanen et al., 2007).

Statistical differences in water quality parameters (temperature, pH, DO, ammonium, nitrite, nitrate, and phosphate) among treatments were tested using a one-way ANOVA and Duncan's multiple-range test in SPSS (IBM SPSS Statistics 19.0, IBM, USA) after verifying the homogeneity of variance using Levene's test.
