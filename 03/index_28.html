Conclusions

This study produced four major advances in understanding the utility of biofloc in for improving biomass yield and ameliorating the accumulation of nitrogen wastes in aquaculture systems.

Firstly, BFT could improve water quality and produce an increase in fish biomass by reducing the dissolved and sedimentary nitrogen.

Secondly, the heterotrophic nitrifiers were absent from the community in BFT, resulting in less efflux of nitrogen to the atmosphere.

Thirdly, the filamentous bacterium formed the major structure of biofloc. Fourthly, Actinobacteria promote the formation of floc and could have secondary benefits of protecting against fish pathogens, though may increase the accumulation of off-flavor in fish tissue.

BFT is a compelling technology that can augment conventional aquaculture with several benefits to the producer and the environment.
