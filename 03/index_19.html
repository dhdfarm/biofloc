Taxonomy-Based Bacterial Community Composition

Bacterial communities significantly differed by sample source and treatment. Proteobacteria predominated in the water environment of CT (61.6%) and included, in order of dominance, the α-class (35.4%), β-class (16.2) and γ-class (8.8%).

Acidobacteria (14.7%), Verrucomicrobia (6.3%) and Bacteroidetes (4.8%) were also abundant members of the water in the CT enclosure.

In contrast, the bacterial communities in BT and BZT showed increased relative abundances of Actinobacteria, Fusobateria, Chloroflexi and Saccharibacteria, with the relative abundance of Proteobacteria decreasing to 33.4% and 30%.

The proportion of Cyanobacteria also decreased from 2.8% in CT to 1.2% and 1% in BT and BTZ, respectively. Intestinal communities differed from those in water and were affected by BFT.

There was a significant difference in the intestinal communities between fish species in CT, where the Gibel carp was dominated by Fusobateria (64.8%) followed by Proteobacteria (30.0%) and the common carp was dominated by Proteobacteria (44.3%) then Fusobateria (28.0%).

Significant differences in intestinal communities were also observed between CT and biofloc treatment in the increase in Actinobacteria. The 50 genera with the greatest differences in relative abundance among treatments were selected for multiple comparisons and presented as a heatmap.

At the genus level, the bacterial communities in the water and intestines of two carp hosts varied in abundance among treatments. These results supported the results of NMDS analyses and community bar plot analysis.

In the first level of cluster tree, the gut microbiota of Gibel carp and common carp in CT clustered by treatment; however, in BT and BZT, water, Gibel carp and common carp clustered according to origin and host at shorter distances.

Gibel carp and common carp from biofloc treated enclosure were clustered in the second level, then clustered with water from biofloc treated enclosure in the third level. The bacterial community of water from CT clustered with water and gut samples from BT and B&ZT.
