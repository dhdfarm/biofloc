The Positive Effect of Biofloc on the Growth Performance of Fish

BTF treatment promoted fish health and yields, based on SGR and FCR, at no extra cost of dietary protein. Similarly, Li et al. (2018) found the protein in fish diet could be reduced to 290 g kg−1 without affecting growth in a biofloc system with Gibel carp.

It has also been shown that biofloc could compensate for 100 g kg−1 dietary protein and maintain or improve growth performance (Mansour and Esteban, 2017). There are several explanations for the benefits of biofloc on growth performance in aquaculture.

Firstly, bacteria in the biofloc predigest fish feed producing higher value nutrients, or scavenge nutrients from fish feces, thereby recycling valuable nutrients to fish (Avnimelech, 1999; Ray et al., 2017).

Biofloc was rich in fatty acids, vitamins, and micronutrients such as manganese, zinc, copper, calcium, sodium, potassium and magnesium (Kuhn et al., 2010; Ju et al., 2008; Long et al., 2015; Maic√° et al., 2012).

Secondly, the build-up of toxic nitrogenous compounds is regulated by biofloc, improving water quality and contributing to fish fitness.

Finally, BFT could modify the microbiota of host intestines, which might improve absorption capacity, growth performance and health of the hosts.
