Tracking Populations of Probable Zoogloea-Forming Microorganisms

The 16S rRNA gene sequence of Zoogloea resiniphila MMB (GenBank: KU321684.1) was used to find the similar nucleotide of OTUs in the local data base of microbiota's 16S rRNA gene sequences by Basic Local Alignment Search Tool (BLAST) (Zhang et al., 1997).

OTU464, OTU726, OTU65, OTU222, OTU2138 and OTU1802 had an expect value lower than e−174. The identities (the number of same nucleotide/ the number of total nucleotide) was from 93% to 92%.

The low similarity demonstrated that Zoogloea resiniphila MMB was absent from the microbial community and revealing that the bioaugmented population did not survive.

OTU464 (f_Rhodocyclaceae) and OUT222 (g_Dechloromonas) was probable zoogloea-forming microorganisms, had sizeable populations in water (0.26% and 0.02%, respectively) and occurred in higher relative abundances than other OTUs in BT and BZT compared to CT.

The neighbor-joining tree showed phylogenetic relationships among the OTU464 and OUT222 and the related bacterial species based on the 16S rRNA gene sequences.
